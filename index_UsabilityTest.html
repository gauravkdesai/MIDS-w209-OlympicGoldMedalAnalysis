1 Introduction

The Origin: The Olympic Games was inspired by the ancient Olympic Games, which were held in Olympia, Greece, from the 8th century BC to the 4th century AD. Baron Pierre de Coubertin founded the International Olympic Committee (IOC) in 1894, leading to the first modern Games in Athens in 1896. The modern Olympic Games or Olympics are leading international sporting events featuring summer and winter sports competitions in which thousands of athletes from around the world participate in a variety of competitions. The Olympic Games are considered the world's foremost sports competition with more than 200 nations participating.The Olympic Games are held every four years, with the Summer and Winter Games alternating by occurring every four years but two years apart.

What to expect: The goal of this project is to understand and identify the patterns for past 120 years Olympic Games and Gold Medals from Country, gemographic, gender, sports, and athlets perspective.

From where did we get the Data:Data are collected from Kaggle. One dataset is 120 years of Olympic histroy: athletes and results. This dataset contains 271116 rows and 15 columns, which covers name, gender, age, height, weight, team, games, year, season, city, sport, event, and Medal. Another dataset is Olympic Sports and Medals, 1896-2014. This dataset contains medal information as well as country, sport, event, GDP per capita, and population information.

Target Audience:The basic users are Media. The intermediate users are sports enthusiasts, athletes. The more advanced users are prospective bidders


2 Overview

Interesting Insights: Since the Olympic Games were first held in 1896, the event has provided a platform for countries to showcase their sporting talent on the world stage. To date, the world has come together to participate in 29 Olympic Games, with the US, Greece, and the UK hosting the most games. Countries in Europe, North America, and Oceania have participated in the most Olympic Games, while with the exception of Egypt and South Africa, African countries have participated in comparatively fewer Olympic Games. The continent has yet to host its first event.


The first Olympic Games featured 12 participant countries, growing significantly in popularity to more than 200 countries in the latest 2016 games, held in Rio de Janeiro. The number of sports, events, and athletes has also grown proportionally to the number of countries over time, from 176 athletes competing across 47 events in 1896 to more than 11,000 athletes competing across 306 events in 2016. Interestingly, world politics also factor prominently into the Olympic Games.


While simply qualifying for the Olympic Games is an achievement in itself, we often associate Olympic success with the number of medals won, and medal counts are a common way of comparing across countries and athletes. The tradition of awarding medals began in the 1904 Summer Olympics in St. Louis, Missouri and, with a few exceptions, gold, silver, and bronze medals are awarded to the winner, first runner-up, and second runner-up for each event respectively.

Currently, the sports that hold the largest number of events, and therefore yield the highest medal counts, include athletics (track and field), swimming, cycing, wrestling, canoeing, shooting, and weight lifting. However, simply increasing the number of countries and events has not translated into success for all countries. Over time, the proportion of countries winning medals to total participant countries has declined significantly, with just 42 percent of countries taking home medals in 2016. More than 35 countries have yet to medal in an Olympic event historically.
